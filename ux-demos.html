UX Redesign
UX AUDIT

Main UX Problems Identified

After analyzing every screen, flow, and interaction in Seder HaDorot, these are the critical issues affecting user experience, engagement, and retention.

Critical
No Onboarding or Context
Users land directly on a long scroll with no explanation of what this is, what they can do, or where to start. High bounce risk for first-time visitors.
Critical
Scroll-Only Navigation
The only way to explore is endless vertical scroll. Era dots are 10px, easy to miss. No filtering, no grid view, no way to jump to specific content types.
High
Detail Panel Overloaded
6 tabs crammed into a 400px slide-in panel. Content hierarchy is flat. Family tree requires a separate floating window — disorienting context switch.
High
Search Is Too Basic
Search only matches names. No filtering by era, role, or relationship. Results just scroll to a card — no preview, no context, no related suggestions.
High
Mobile Experience Broken
On mobile: sidebar and era dots disappear entirely, cards stack in single column with no tree visual. The timeline metaphor collapses.
Medium
Empty States Everywhere
Most tabs show italic placeholder text ("content will be filled later"). This erodes trust and makes the product feel incomplete and unreliable.
Medium
No Cross-Linking or Discovery
After viewing a person, there's no "related figures" or "same era" suggestions. Users must manually navigate back and hunt for connections.
Medium
Accessibility Gaps
No skip-to-content link, no ARIA landmarks, no focus management on panel open/close, low contrast on muted text, tiny era dot touch targets (10px).
DEMO 1

Landing & Onboarding Experience

Replace the bare scroll-into-timeline with a purposeful hero section that explains the product, sets expectations, and gives users multiple entry points into the content.

CURRENT: No landing

Users see the title "סדר הדורות" and immediately face a long vertical scroll. No context, no CTA, no way to understand what they're looking at or how to use it. The first visible content depends entirely on scroll position.

PROPOSED: Hero + Era Carousel

A hero section with value proposition, key stats, and clear CTAs. Below it, an era carousel lets users pick their entry point. The scroll timeline begins after — users arrive with context and intent.

DEMO 3

Redesigned Person Detail Panel

Instead of a narrow 400px slide-in with 6 confusing tabs, use a wider panel with cleaner hierarchy: identity at top, family chips for quick navigation, fewer tabs (merged sources + notes), and a "related figures" section for discovery.

KEY CHANGES
What's Different
Horizontal avatar + name — saves vertical space, feels more like a profile header
Family chips — clickable inline navigation to relatives, always visible
4 tabs instead of 6 — merged Sources + Notes, family tree embedded
Related figures — discovery section at bottom drives exploration
Wider panel (440px) — more breathing room for content and family tree

PRIORITY ROADMAP

Implementation Roadmap

Divided into three phases based on impact, effort, and dependency order. Phase 1 addresses the highest-impact issues that cause user drop-off.

Phase 1: Must Fix Now (Week 1-2)
Hero/Landing section — Add value proposition, stats, and CTA above the timeline. First-time visitors need context before content. Estimated: 4h.
Era carousel entry — Horizontal scrollable cards below hero. Click jumps to era. Reduces cognitive load and gives intentional navigation. Estimated: 3h.
Empty state upgrade — Replace italic placeholders with meaningful fallbacks: show family links, era context, or "contribute" CTA when content is missing. Estimated: 2h.
Mobile bottom navigation — Era selector as a swipeable bottom drawer or tab bar when sidebar/dots are hidden. Estimated: 4h.
Accessibility basics — Skip-to-content link, ARIA landmarks on main/aside/nav, focus trap in modals, visible focus rings, 4.5:1 contrast on muted text. Estimated: 3h.
Phase 2: Should Improve Next (Week 3-5)
Grid view mode — Add era-focused grid alongside the timeline scroll. Toggle between "timeline" and "browse" modes via a toolbar button. Estimated: 6h.
Enhanced search with filters — Role chips, era tags, grouped results, related-figure suggestions, and inline preview summaries. Estimated: 5h.
Detail panel redesign — Wider panel (440px), horizontal header layout, family chips, merged Sources+Notes tab, inline family tree. Estimated: 6h.
Cross-linking & "Related Figures" — Automatic section at bottom of detail panel showing same-era figures, siblings, and people referenced in the same sources. Estimated: 4h.
Breadcrumb navigation — Show path: Home > Era > Person. Click to jump back without browser back button. Estimated: 2h.
Phase 3: Nice to Have Later (Month 2+)
Interactive D3 timeline bar — Replace static sidebar with zoomable timeline showing lifespans as overlapping horizontal bars. Estimated: 8h.
First-visit tooltip tour — Guided walkthrough for new users explaining era dots, search, family trees, and contribution flow. Estimated: 4h.
Bookmarks/favorites — Save figures to a personal collection using localStorage or Supabase profiles. Estimated: 3h.
Share a figure — Shareable URLs with Open Graph metadata (title, summary, era) for social sharing. Estimated: 3h.
Comparison view — Select two figures to see side-by-side timeline, family, and events. Estimated: 6h.
Keyboard shortcuts — J/K to navigate cards, / to open search, Esc cascading close, arrow keys in family tree. Estimated: 3h.
